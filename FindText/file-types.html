Why OCR helps

Recover words that normal file search cannot see.

Many scanned PDFs and image files look readable to people but contain no selectable text. OCR bridges that gap by recognizing printed words inside the image.

Search scanned research

Use OCR for newspaper archives, historical references, book excerpts, classroom material, manuals, and printed documents you scanned years ago.

Find hidden text faster

Instead of opening image files one by one, search for a name, place, topic, quote, invoice number, or printed phrase across your selected folders.

Helpful for English print

OCR is especially useful for English printed pages such as newspapers, forms, old books, typed letters, receipts, and scanned office paperwork.

Keep the workflow local

Search your own selected folders on your Mac, preview matching passages, and open the original document only when you have found the right one.
